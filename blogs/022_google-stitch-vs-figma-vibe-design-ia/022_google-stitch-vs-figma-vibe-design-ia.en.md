On March 18th, Google updated Stitch — its AI design tool. The next day, Figma's stock dropped 8.8%. Not because of a scandal or bad earnings. Because of a free tool that does what Figma does but with AI and without charging a cent.

A few months ago we talked about Vibe Coding — the idea that you can program by describing what you want in natural language. Karpathy coined the term and it became a revolution. Now Google just did the same thing with design: Vibe Design. You describe how you want your app to look and the AI generates it. No wireframes, no Figma, no design skills needed.

## What is Google Stitch

Stitch is a Google Labs tool that uses Gemini 2.5 Pro to generate complete interfaces from text, sketches, or screenshots. It's been around for a while, but the March update turned it into something entirely different:

- Vibe Design — describe what you want ("a minimalist landing page for a productivity SaaS with three-tier pricing") and Stitch generates the complete UI. No wireframes, no manual layout, no artboards.

- Voice Canvas — speak directly to the canvas. Tell it what to change, it asks clarifying questions, critiques your design in real time, and generates updates while you talk. You can create a complete landing page from a conversation.

- React/TypeScript code by default — everything Stitch generates includes functional code, not just static mockups. You can go from idea to working prototype without opening an editor.

- Infinite canvas — an expandable workspace where ideas grow from sketches to functional prototypes in one place. No tool switching.

- Agent Manager — an AI agent that tracks your complete project, lets you explore multiple design directions simultaneously, and maintains the full history.

- Design System Import — import your existing design system from a URL or DESIGN.md file so generations respect your brand.

- Direct Figma export — if you need to refine in Figma, export with preserved layers and components.

And the most important detail: it's completely free. Figma charges between $12 and $75 per user per month.

## Vibe Design: the natural extension of Vibe Coding

In February 2025, Andrej Karpathy tweeted about "vibe coding" — the idea that you can program without knowing how to program, just by describing what you want. A year later, the concept evolved into context engineering and agentic engineering. Now Google applies the same paradigm to design.

Vibe Coding: "I want an app that does X" → the model generates the code.

Vibe Design: "I want it to look like this" → the model generates the interface.

The pattern is the same: AI replaces technical skill with intention. You don't need to know CSS for your app to look professional. You don't need to master Figma to have a design that competes with Silicon Valley startups. You just need to know what you want.

And this is exactly what we've been building with AI agents. An agent that codes (like Claude Code or Composer 2) + a tool that designs (like Stitch) = a single founder can do the work of a complete team. No hiring designers. No learning Figma. No waiting weeks for mockups.

## Why did Figma's stock crash?

Figma trades 85% below its peak. And now drops another 8.8% in a day. Is it rational?

Partially yes. When the most resource-rich company on the planet decides your product should be free, you have a problem. Google doesn't need to monetize Stitch — it can give it away as part of its AI ecosystem. Figma needs to charge to survive.

But the drop also reflects exaggerated fear. Stitch is excellent at what it does: rapid brainstorming, initial prototypes, exploring ideas. Where Figma remains irreplaceable is in precision: pixel-perfect design, complex design systems, large team collaboration, developer handoff.

The analogy is exactly the same as with Vibe Coding: it's an incredible tool for 80% of the journey, but the final 20% — refinement, precision, edge cases — still needs human hands. For now.

## What this means for entrepreneurs

If you're a solo founder or small team in LATAM, the Stitch + code agents combination is a game-changer. Think about it:

- Before: hire a designer ($1,000-3,000/month) + wait 2-4 weeks for mockups + back and forth with revisions

- Now: describe what you want to Stitch → get a prototype in minutes → export the React code → hand it to your AI agent to implement

The complete cycle from "idea → design → working code" that used to take weeks can now happen in hours. For free.

It's not perfect. Generated designs will need adjustments. Code will need refactoring. But as a starting point, it's infinitely better than starting from scratch. And for MVPs, landing pages, and validation prototypes, it's more than enough.

## The trend nobody can ignore

In the same week: Cursor launches a code AI model that costs 10x less than the competition. Google launches a free design tool that tanks Figma's stock. The pattern is clear: AI is collapsing the cost of professional tools.

For entrepreneurs in LATAM, this is particularly relevant. Tools previously reserved for teams with first-world budgets are now accessible to anyone with a computer and an internet connection. You don't need $10,000/month in development and design tools. You need to know what you want to build.

From vibe coding to vibe design. From expensive models to cheap models. From paid tools to free tools. The direction is clear: the barrier to entry for building digital products is disappearing. The question is no longer whether you have the tools — it's whether you have the idea.
